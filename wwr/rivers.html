Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Nestled in the heart of the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return, the Salmon River, affectionately called the "River of No Return," beckons with its rugged beauty. Surrounded by elk, deer, bears, and bighorn sheep, and framed by towering ponderosa pines, this undammed river boasts rapids ranging from class I to IV.

As one of the longest undammed rivers in the United States, the Salmon offers a dynamic experience. Early summer brings higher, faster, and colder waters, perfect for thrill-seekers. Late summer, on the other hand, slows the pace, creating warmer currents ideal for families with children who love to swim. Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Winding through central Utah's canyons, the Green River traverses some of the most remote landscapes, flowing toward the mighty Colorado River and the sea. Embraced by high grey stone cliffs and captivating desert vistas, this 84-mile journey from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid, just outside Green River Utah, has gained fame for its remarkable scenery, geological wonders, traces of prehistoric and historic human activity, and exhilarating whitewater rapids.

Desolation Canyon, a Class II/III rated adventure, unfolds over six days, offering a captivating blend of outstanding landscapes and thrilling whitewater opportunities. Recognized as a National Landmark, the canyon's designation is a testament to its unique features and rich historical narrative. Embark on a voyage through Desolation Canyon, where every twist and turn reveals the captivating tale of this extraordinary natural wonder.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

As the grand daddy of all canyons and river trips, a visit to the Grand Canyon is an unforgettable experience. Grand Canyon river rafting seamlessly blends world-class whitewater excitement with awe-inspiring scenery, creating a truly unparalleled adventure. The canyon not only features exhilarating rapids ranging from Class I to X but also unveils side canyons and ancient Indian ruins, accessible solely by river.

The Grand Canyon boasts a distinctive river rating scale, ranging from I to X (1 to 10), accommodating the diverse rapids and river variations within its majestic depths. While this scale is unique, many are more familiar with the traditional I - V river rating, and we often refer to both, ensuring that all adventurers can gauge the thrill that awaits in this iconic natural wonder. Embark on a Grand Canyon river journey, where the fusion of whitewater excitement and breathtaking landscapes creates memories that last a lifetime.
